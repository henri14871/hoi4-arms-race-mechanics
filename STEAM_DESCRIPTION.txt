[h1]Arms Race Mechanics[/h1]

[b]Keep the world technologically competitive.[/b]

If you are tired of 1944 wars where major powers still have bizarre holes in air, armor, radar, or naval tech, this mod is for you.

Arms Race Mechanics gives AI nations a credible tech baseline based on industry, military profile, and war pressure. Major powers stay near the curve. Regional powers stay dangerous in their strongest branches. Minor countries stay relevant without becoming ahistorical superpowers.

[b]Your research still matters.[/b] ARM is AI-only by default and keeps auto-grants behind the frontier, so the player still wins through better timing, specialization, and planning.

[h2]What It Does[/h2]
[list]
[*] [b]Stops absurd tech gaps.[/b] Majors no longer fall years behind in branches they should clearly support.
[*] [b]Keeps wars competitive.[/b] Countries under pressure get catch-up help instead of collapsing into tech irrelevance.
[*] [b]Makes minors matter.[/b] Smaller nations stay useful and dangerous in the areas they can realistically sustain.
[*] [b]Preserves player agency.[/b] The mod raises the baseline without replacing the value of smart research choices.
[/list]

[h2]How It Works[/h2]
ARM scores each nation by power, economy, science, mobilization, resources, and war posture, then places it into a tier with a target lag behind the frontier.

Four [b]branch competence[/b] tracks (Land, Air, Naval, Industry) decide [i]which[/i] techs a country can plausibly receive. A naval power gets naval tech. A land-focused industrial state gets armor and artillery. Countries do not get random filler tech they cannot support.

Quarterly grants, war catch-up, faction support, and puppet sharing keep the world closer to the curve while avoiding full homogenization.

[h2]Features[/h2]
[list]
[*] Six power tiers from Micro to Superpower
[*] Branch-based tech granting for Land, Air, Naval, and Industry
[*] Quarterly tech grants with configurable caps
[*] War, faction, and desperation catch-up bonuses
[*] Anti-death-spiral support for late-game relevance
[*] Puppet tech sharing and research-speed spirits
[*] DLC-aware grants for Man the Guns, No Step Back, and By Blood Alone
[*] 13 game rules for scope, intensity, and behavior
[*] AI-only by default, with opt-in for player nations
[/list]
